Why These Journals?

This monitor follows six core hearing-science journals because they regularly publish work that connects auditory theory, clinical audiology, speech perception, cochlear implants, hearing aids, and listening technologies.

  • JASAFoundational acoustics, psychoacoustics, speech communication, and auditory perception.
  • JASA Express LettersRapid reports across acoustics, speech, and hearing-related auditory science.
  • Trends in HearingOpen-access research on hearing, communication, and translational auditory science.
  • JAROMechanistic auditory research from the cochlea through neural and perceptual systems.
  • Ear and HearingClinical audiology, diagnostics, rehabilitation, amplification, and patient outcomes.
  • Hearing ResearchBasic and applied auditory research spanning biology, neuroscience, and technology-relevant mechanisms.

Together, these sources capture basic and applied-basic research that is especially important for hearing technologies, including better models, measurements, devices, fitting strategies, and real-world listening outcomes.
